About Quito

Quito, officially known as San Francisco de Quito, is the capital of the Republic of Ecuador. It also acts as the capital of the Province of Pichincha and the cantonal nucleus of the Metropolitan District of Quito. Located in the north of the inter-Andean region of Ecuador, in the basin of the Guayllabamba River, on the western slopes of the Pichincha stratovolcano, with an average altitude of 2,850 meters above sea level, it is the constitutional capital with the highest altitude in the world.

Population: 2.8 million

Language: Spanish
